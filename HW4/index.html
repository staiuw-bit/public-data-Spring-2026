website title Broken English by Rupi Kaur captures a powerful and relatable moment—a daughter witnessing her mother being judged for how she speaks English. Despite her multilingual ability, the mother is unfairly seen as less capable simply because her English isn’t “perfect.” The poem highlights how language can shape perceptions of intelligence and belonging, offering a thought-provoking critique of the standards we often take for granted.
